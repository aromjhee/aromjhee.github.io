Hello, I'm Arom Jhee.

I'm a software developer

and a former restaurateur who have experience operating

~$1.2million in sales every quarter with 30% profit.

"What are you doing working in a restaurant?"

This is the question I would often hear from peers and families, and sometimes even from my loyal customers. I achieved my life-long goal of becoming a restaurant owner through determination and many self-sacrifices. But when that failed, I thought maybe there is some truth to what people see in me and that there is a career more fitting.

How I got into software development

I felt at home when I discovered programming, because I found all that I loved about the food business in the coding world as well. For example, feeling the “flow” of the operation. I knew exactly what was going on inside the kitchen even while I was by the entrance greeting customers. I'm able to do this as well as a software engineer where queried data are sent, in some data structure, through some functions, and rendered on a specific location on the browser. This skill enables me to find bottlenecks and make improvements, which can be so satisfying. Additionally, by grasping that bird’s-eye view, I can make my code more efficient, more readable, and optimized.

My experience in the coding boot camp

I have ample amount of experience in JavaScript, Python, React, Docker, Flask, SQLAlchemy, PostgreSQL, Node.js, Express, HTML, CSS. My proudest achievement is the unit tests I wrote for my project C.A.R.L and imma-Zone. I’m able to debug faster and ensure code integrity with test driven development and all it requires is discipline. TDD resonates with my core in that I should be disciplined and write sustainable, error-proven code.

What I bring to the table

As a former restaurant owner, I have a deep understanding of what it means to work in a fast paced environment. I'm flexible and responsive to requirement changes, attuned with customer needs, and able to interconnect every aspect of a product to a business idea. As a software developer, I’m not afraid to dig through the documentation and ask questions. My team can rely on me to carry my weight and always deliver. I want to be part of a team to create a synergy to end a sprint cycle with high-fives and fist-bumps.
